02 Research

My work sits at the intersection of computational social science and computer-mediated communication, and centres on a single question: how do digital technologies shape human life?

Ⅰ

Technology & social time

The temporal rhythms of digital media use: how they form, and with what consequences for individuals and societies.

Ⅱ

Platform design & engagement

How the design features of platforms steer users’ interactions, from engagement bait to geolocation cues that polarise group behaviour.

Ⅲ

Technology & lifestyle

How emerging technologies, most recently generative AI, re-pattern the textures of everyday life.

Methodologically I work with large-scale digital trace data, natural experiments, causal inference, and large language models. My research has appeared in Computers in Human Behavior, Social Media + Society, Telematics and Informatics, Public Relations Review, Environmental Communication, Journal of Health Communication, and Journalism, among others.

04 Software

Beyond papers, I build research infrastructure: platforms that put computational methods and AI in the hands of researchers.

ChatDMDI

An integrated AI platform I designed, built, and maintain for the School of Journalism and Communication at CUHK, where faculty and students work with multiple large language models side by side for research and learning.

Visit platform

Social Movement Lens

A human–AI collaborative research engine for studying global social movements. Expert-coded data meets semantic search, geospatial and temporal visualisation, and an AI agent for conversational analysis.
